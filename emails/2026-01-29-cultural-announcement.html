How cultural context shapes symptom expression, diagnosis, and clinical care in psychotic disorders.

August Natterer, Hexenkopf (Witch’s Head), 1915

Learning objectives

—Understand how historical and cultural contexts shape psychotic disorders
—Recognize cultural influences on symptom expression and meaning
—Apply cultural insights to help-seeking and treatment planning

G. Eric Jarvis, MD is Professor of Psychiatry at McGill University and Director of the Cultural Consultation Service and First Episode Psychosis Program at the Jewish General Hospital. His work focuses on cultural influences on psychosis, access to care, and early intervention.
